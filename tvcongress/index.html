Congressional TV Ads

Tools and analysis by Dan Ancona for Way to Win, with data from Dailykos Elections and AdImpact

The 2020 fight for the US Congress ended with Democrats losing all 27 of the the Cook Political-rated D and R tossups, and only narrowly retaining their majority. Centrist Democrats were quick to blame the left flank; particularly activists that had raised the topic of police funding in the wake of a series of shocking summary state executions and Democratic Socialists. Republicans had announced this name-calling strategy early in the cycle, and it seems possible that a more aggressive strategy to combat it should have been possible. However, the lack of investment in research and evaluation (particularly critical when communicating with the diverse audiences that proved pivotal in many districts) has resulted both in numerous losses and a lack of clear signals on paths forward. To meet this, Way to Win commissioned a study of the overall message landscape. These interactive tools were one result.

Many thanks to Dailykos Elections Data for their excellent work, and to AdImpact for allowing these views of their datasets to be made public. If you'd like to inquire about their service or obtaining copies of their data for post-cycle analysis, please contact John Link directly.

The interactive tables are not mobile adapted. Please view them on a larger screen.
