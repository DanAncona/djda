Congressional TV Ads

Tools and analysis by Dan Ancona for Way to Win, with data from Dailykos Elections and AdImpact

The 2020 fight for the US Congress ended with Democrats losing all twenty seven of the the Cook Political-rated D and R tossups, and only narrowly retaining their majority. Centrist Democrats were quick to blame the left. Yet Republicans had announced this name-calling strategy early in the cycle, and it seems possible that a more aggressive strategy to combat it should have been possible - an argument advanced by Justice Democrats and other groups in their preliminary 2020 analysis. It seems as if there were several systemic-level suboptimizations, but one seems to have been underinvestment in the kind of research and evaluation. Research is particularly critical when communicating with diverse audiences. These segments of voters proved pivotal in many districts, yet were patronizingly referred to as "mobilization" segments.

The good news is that Democratic leadership has already begun taken action to address these issues. When Rep Maloney was elected to run the DCCC, one of his first actions was to end the blacklist that had limited new consulting firms from competing. But given the historic challenges Democrats face in 2022, it's going to take a lot more than this. To support this effort, Way to Win commissioned a study of the overall message landscape 2022. These interactive tools were one result.

Many thanks to Dailykos Elections Data for their excellent work, and to AdImpact for allowing these views of their datasets to be made public. If you'd like to inquire about their ad intelligence or obtaining copies of their data for post-cycle analysis, please contact John Link at AdImpact directly.
